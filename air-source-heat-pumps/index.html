What Is an Air Source Heat Pump?

An air source heat pump extracts heat from the outside air — even in cold temperatures — and uses it to heat your home and provide hot water. It works like a fridge in reverse, moving heat rather than generating it, which is why it uses so much less energy than a traditional boiler.

Air source heat pumps connect to your existing radiators or underfloor heating and include a hot water cylinder. They require no groundworks, can usually be installed in one to two days, and are suitable for most Aberdeen homes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to install an air source heat pump in Scotland?

Installation typically costs between £8,000 and £15,000. Most Aberdeen homeowners qualify for the Home Energy Scotland Grant of up to £7,500, bringing the net cost down significantly. An interest-free loan of up to £7,500 is also available.

Do heat pumps work in cold weather?

Yes. Modern systems are designed to operate in temperatures as low as -20°C. During the January 2025 cold snap, 9 in 10 heat pump owners reported strong performance in freezing and snowy conditions — so you can be confident your Aberdeen home will stay warm all winter.

Do I need planning permission?

In most cases, no. Air source heat pumps are classed as permitted development in Scotland. Exceptions apply for listed buildings and conservation areas — we'll confirm during your free survey.

How long does installation take?

Most installations are completed in one to two days with minimal disruption to your home.
