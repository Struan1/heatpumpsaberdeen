1. Home Energy Scotland Grant & Loan

The Home Energy Scotland Grant & Loan is the main funding scheme for Aberdeen homeowners and is available regardless of your income. You do not need to be on benefits to qualify — any homeowner in Scotland can apply.

How Much Can You Get?

  • Up to £7,500 as a free grant — never needs to be repaid
  • Up to £9,000 grant for rural and island Aberdeenshire homes
  • Optional interest-free loan of up to £7,500 at 0%
  • Combined maximum of £15,000 (or £16,500 for rural homes)

Who Qualifies?

  • You own your home and it is your primary residence
  • The property is an existing building — not a new build
  • The heat pump provides 100% of your heating and hot water
  • Your installer is MCS certified (all our engineers are)
  • You have a valid EPC or Home Energy Scotland assessment

Funding is allocated on a first-come, first-served basis, so it's worth applying sooner rather than later.

2. Warmer Homes Scotland

If you're on a low income or certain qualifying benefits, you may be eligible for Warmer Homes Scotland — a Scottish Government scheme that can cover the full cost of heat pump installation and other energy efficiency improvements.

How Much Can You Get?

  • Eligible households can receive £10,000 or more
  • Can cover a free heat pump installation
  • May also include insulation and other improvements

Who Qualifies?

  • You must be a homeowner or private tenant
  • The property is your primary residence
  • You are struggling to afford to heat your home
  • Someone in your household is 75+, terminally ill, or on qualifying benefits

Is It Worth Getting a Heat Pump in Scotland?

With up to £7,500 in free grant funding available, the financial case for installing a heat pump in Aberdeen is stronger than ever. Heat pumps are up to 4x more efficient than a gas boiler, cutting heating bills by 30–50% and reducing your carbon emissions by over 70%. Scotland generates over 85% of its electricity from renewable sources — meaning your heat pump will run on some of the greenest electricity in the UK.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for the heat pump grant in Scotland?

Any homeowner in Scotland can apply for the Home Energy Scotland Grant — you do not need to be on benefits. You must own your home, live in it as your primary residence, and have the heat pump installed by an MCS certified installer. Rural homes qualify for a higher grant of up to £9,000.

Do you have to pay back a government grant for a heat pump?

No — the grant element does not need to be repaid. The optional loan element is repaid over up to 10 years at 0% interest. You can choose to take the grant only, the loan only, or both.

Is it worth getting a heat pump in Scotland?

Yes — particularly with up to £7,500 in free grant funding. Heat pumps cut heating bills by 30–50%, reduce carbon emissions by over 70% and last 20–25 years. Scotland's renewable electricity grid makes them even more environmentally beneficial than elsewhere in the UK.

How much is the heat pump grant in Scotland in 2026?

Up to £7,500 as a free grant, or £9,000 for rural properties. An additional interest-free loan of up to £7,500 is available, giving a combined maximum of £15,000 (or £16,500 for rural homes).

Can I get a free heat pump in Aberdeen?

If you're on qualifying benefits or a low income, Warmer Homes Scotland can cover the full cost — £10,000 or more. If you don't qualify for Warmer Homes Scotland, the Home Energy Scotland Grant of up to £7,500 is available to all homeowners regardless of income.
