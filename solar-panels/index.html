Do Solar Panels Work in Aberdeen?

This is the question we get asked most — and the answer is a clear yes. Solar panels generate electricity from daylight, not direct sunshine. Aberdeen receives plenty of daylight throughout the year, and the cooler Scottish climate can actually improve panel efficiency compared to hotter regions.

The proof is in the numbers. During the 2018 summer heatwave, Aberdeen's solar panels generated 136% of average household electricity needs. Even in winter, your system will generate a meaningful contribution to your energy use — and in spring and summer, it can cover a significant portion of your electricity bill entirely.

Don't let Scotland's reputation for grey skies put you off. Homeowners across Aberdeenshire are already benefiting from solar, and the combination of falling installation costs, 0% VAT and the Smart Export Guarantee makes now an excellent time to make the switch.

Once your MCS certified system is installed, you can register for the Smart Export Guarantee and get paid for surplus electricity you export back to the grid.

Maximise Your Savings With Solar and Battery Storage

Solar panels generate most of their electricity during the day — but most of your energy use happens in the morning and evening. Adding a battery storage system stores your surplus solar electricity so you can use it when you need it most, rather than exporting it to the grid at a lower rate.

Paired with a heat pump, solar panels and battery storage can dramatically reduce your home's reliance on grid electricity — cutting your bills further and future-proofing your home against rising energy prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to install solar panels in Scotland?

The average cost is around £6,961 for a 4kW system in Scotland as of 2026. All installations benefit from 0% VAT until March 2027, and a Home Energy Scotland interest-free loan of up to £15,000 is available for most homeowners.

Is it worth getting solar panels in Scotland?

Yes — solar panels save the average Scottish homeowner around £660 a year on electricity bills, with a payback period of 8–9 years. After that, you're generating free electricity for 15+ years. Aberdeen's solar panels even generated 136% of average household electricity needs during the 2018 summer heatwave.

What is the 20% rule for solar panels?

It's a guideline suggesting panels should cover at least 20% of your roof area to generate a meaningful return. In practice, our MCS certified engineers will survey your roof and design a system sized specifically to your home's energy needs and available roof space.

Do solar panels work in Aberdeen's climate?

Yes. Solar panels generate electricity from daylight, not just direct sunshine. Aberdeen receives enough daylight throughout the year to make solar a sound investment, and Scotland's cooler temperatures can actually improve panel efficiency.

Can I combine solar panels with a heat pump?

Absolutely — it's one of the most effective combinations for cutting energy bills. Solar generates free electricity during the day for your heat pump to use, and battery storage lets you use that electricity in the evenings too. We install all three and can design a fully integrated renewable energy system for your home.
