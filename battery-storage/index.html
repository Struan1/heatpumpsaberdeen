How Does Solar Battery Storage Work?

Solar panels generate electricity during daylight hours — but most households use the bulk of their energy in the morning and evening, when the sun isn't shining. Without a battery, that surplus daytime electricity is exported back to the grid at a relatively low rate, meaning you're not getting full value from your system.

A solar battery stores your surplus electricity during the day so you can use it in the evenings, overnight or on cloudy days — instead of buying it back from the grid at a higher rate. The result is a dramatic increase in how much of your own solar electricity you actually use, and a significant reduction in your energy bills.

Modern batteries can also be programmed to charge overnight using cheap off-peak electricity tariffs — such as Octopus Energy's Agile tariff — giving you an additional layer of savings even during periods of low solar generation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is solar battery storage worth it in Scotland?

Yes. Without a battery, most homes use only around 30% of the electricity their solar panels generate. Adding storage increases this to over 80%, with homeowners saving an average of £840 a year. Battery prices are now 40% cheaper than in 2022, making it a strong investment.

How long do solar batteries last?

Most modern lithium batteries last 10–15 years and come with a 10-year manufacturer warranty. After the warranty period, batteries typically retain around 80% of their original capacity, continuing to provide useful storage beyond that.

Can I add battery storage to my existing solar panels?

Yes — most existing solar PV systems can be retrofitted with battery storage. We'll assess your setup during your free survey and recommend the best option for your system.

Do I need solar panels to install a battery?

No. A standalone battery can be charged overnight using a cheap off-peak electricity tariff, saving you money even without solar panels. Many Aberdeen homeowners do this alongside smart tariffs like Octopus Agile.

Can I get a grant for battery storage in Scotland?

There's no standalone grant for battery storage, but the Home Energy Scotland interest-free loan of up to £15,000 covers combined solar and battery installations. All installations also benefit from 0% VAT until March 2027.
